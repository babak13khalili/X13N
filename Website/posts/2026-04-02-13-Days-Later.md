title: 13 Days Later
written: 02-04-2026
---

**Thursday – 14:31**

Today is cloudy.
Yesterday was bright and sunny.

It has been 13 days since the last time I wrote.
Two weeks.
I did not notice the passage of time at all, and I cannot believe how quickly these 13 days went by.
Thirteen days into our new year, one whose arrival I did not even get to truly feel.

During this short period, once again, many things happened, events that I am still trying to process and understand, while new ones continue to unfold without giving me any pause.

My father’s birthday passed, as well as the birthday of another relative.
The birthdays of a few old friends.
A trip with the university.
And the migration of close friends who used to live here, near me, to another European country.
And once again, the experience of feeling even more alone.

The 13th day of the year, which has always been a meaningful, beautiful day in Iranian culture, is now passing in complete solitude, far from any celebration, far from friends and family, far from gatherings and nature.

Since my teenage years, I have had a particular sensitivity toward the number 13.
I have never found a clear reason or philosophy behind it, but the fact that my birth date, in the Persian calendar, aligns with the 13th day of the third month has always felt like an intriguing numerical coincidence.
It made me more attentive to this number,
more aware of it than usual in my everyday life.

Because of this, whenever and wherever I encounter the number 13, it brings me a brief moment of pause and reflection.

And now, today, on the 13th day of the new Iranian year,
once again I skipped my class.
Once again, I am submerged in thoughts and stress.
Once again, anxiety.
Once again, rumination.

An attempt to process and understand everything,
almost futile.

The volume of events and their weight feel beyond comprehension.
Concentration has become something almost unattainable these days.
No matter how much I try to give myself time, space, and patience, to see myself, to feel myself,
it is as if I not only fail, but become even more entangled, heavier, more complex.

Every direction I look reveals an endless sea of ambiguity, fear, and worry.
And I, on a floating piece of wood, do not know how long I can rely on it just to stay alive.

On one hand, I do everything that needs to be done, almost flawlessly, automatically.
On the other hand, I have almost lost my presence of mind, my ability to truly feel and grasp the moment.

I constantly suffer, yet I do not express it.
I do not even see much value in expressing it.

The challenges, worries, anxieties, and sadness are one thing,
but not knowing the solution, the path forward, the way to improve, is far worse.

I tell myself, ask for help, talk, express everything in your mind, no matter how dark or heavy.
But before I can act on it, I stop.

Why should others have to carry my burdens, my anxieties, my depression,
when in the end, they cannot really change anything,
and they themselves are already dealing with their own lives?
Would I not just be adding weight, maybe even guilt, to something they never asked for?

Nothing gets resolved.

The fear of an uncertain future still prevents me from enjoying the present,
this moment, this position, all the achievements and blessings I already have.

I am still full of life.
Full of desire to act.
Full of excitement to learn and experience.

But my mind, my fears, my thoughts, consume all my energy
and block productivity,
block focus.

Time moves forward.
Life flows on, mercilessly, waiting for no one.
It does not stop.

And whether I want it or not, I am condemned to continue.
I am not dissatisfied with continuing,
not at all.

But these tangled, disturbing thoughts make everything infinitely harder.

Despite all these personal and external struggles, I continue.
I still try to laugh, to joke, to enjoy moments, to be helpful in society and among friends.
I still try to be useful, reliable, a source of warmth.
Cheerful, light, energetic.

Even though inside, I carry a storm.
Even though from the outside, I receive very little of that same energy back.

Still, I continue.

And at the same time, I try, deeply, to extract something from this path, from these struggles.
To say something. To create something.

Who knows where we will end up?
Who even knows where “the end” is?

No matter how much we plan or set goals,
in the end, it feels like we are all just moving forward to see what happens.

Thirteen days of the new Iranian year have passed,
and I still have not gotten used to naming this new year.

Many more sets of 13 days will pass,
and I will still be in the process of trying to understand myself,
life,
and how it all works.

**2 April 2026 – 15:15**
**Home, Groningen**
