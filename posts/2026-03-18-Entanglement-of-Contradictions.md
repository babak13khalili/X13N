---
title: Entanglement of Contradictions
written: 18-03-2026
---

**Wednesday – 12:58**

I am caught in a deeply strange contradiction.

On one hand, I profoundly need solitude.
From the bottom of my heart, I want to disappear from the stage of life.
To sink into silence and stillness, and not worry about any event, judgment, or thought.
To simply pause, and drift into reflection.
No one to know me.
No one to wait for me.
Nothing expected of me, no responsibility to carry.
Pure stillness and slowness.
Ample time to process events and my inner states.
Which, of course, is not possible.

On the other hand, the most toxic and harmful thing for me right now is solitude.

One day, I become intensely alone; the weight of the world’s sorrow takes over my entire being, and it leads nowhere—no resolution, no relief.
Even the knot in my throat refuses to break, so that at least some of the pressure could be released.

Thoughts, fears, anxiety, and grief consume me like a black hole, and no struggle or effort seems to offer a way out.

Another day, I become surrounded—filled with conversations and people.
Sometimes even intentionally, I say yes to every invitation and plan, just to spend more and more time with others, to avoid being alone, to keep my mind from being entirely under my own control.
In the end, late at night, against my own will, I return home alone, face my solitude, and with overwhelming exhaustion, think only of sleep and rest—so I can begin again tomorrow.

Crowdedness until the very last moments of the day.

Time passes more easily.
The mind feels lighter.
But less gets done.
Fewer things move forward.
There is less room left for personal work.

A strange contradiction.
A need and desire for absolute solitude, yet at the same time an awareness of its harm.

And still, I have not found my truly safe people, or a space where I can express myself fully, without fear or concern.
Perhaps I never will.
Perhaps I never should fully reveal my true self.

What would happen if others knew how much darkness, sadness, and depression pass through the depths of my thoughts and emotions?
Would they be afraid, withdraw, see me as weak—and leave me even more alone?

And yet, I cannot ignore or suppress my emotional and psychological needs.
So far, I have found no solution, no help.

The friends I have back in my own country, even through distance and digital connection, were to a large extent that safe space for me—easing loneliness and homesickness.
But for some time now, they have had no access to the internet, and I am completely cut off from them.

Tonight, without realizing it beforehand, it was Chaharshanbe Suri.
I am not particularly a person of rituals or special occasions, except for a few rare and meaningful ones.
But the fact that I had completely forgotten this day and its date was painful.
How far and how lost had I become, to forget such a day?
And how disordered and sorrowful everything must be, that this historic and memorable occasion did not even carry the feeling of its arrival.

Now, how difficult it is to explain this day—its meaning and emotional weight—to my new, non-Iranian friends, so that perhaps they might understand even a fraction of it.

How heavy and strange it is that we are in the final days of the year, and instead of joy, hope, ease, and a sense of renewal, we feel tired, sad, angry, hopeless, restless, and depressed.

Unlike previous years, this year I was not alone on Chaharshanbe Suri.
I spent time talking and being with many friends.
I was not caught up in work, projects, and deadlines like before—something I had always envied others for, while they celebrated and I stayed behind, working alone.

This year, I was not alone.
I was not at home working on a project.
And that, for me, was something deep and significant.
After years, on this night, I was outside, among friends—yet still unfortunate, still sad, tired, waiting, anxious.

How far can a human endure psychological and emotional pressure, and still continue?

Outwardly, I seem to function strongly, even powerfully, in this regard.

But sometimes I tell myself—I wish I didn’t.
I wish I wasn’t strong.
I wish I could break somewhere, let it all out.
I wish I wasn’t so skilled at hiding my inner state and emotions.
I wish I could collapse, cry, be held—
for a long time.

Without fear of judgment.
Without fear of appearing weak in the eyes of others.

I wish I were simply weak,
and didn’t have to carry this extra burden of holding myself together.
