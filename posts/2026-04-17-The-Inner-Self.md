---
title: The Inner Self
written: 17-04-2026
---

**Thursday – 00:16**

For years, I felt the presence of another person living within me, someone who could have replaced everything I currently am.
Perhaps entirely different, worlds apart.

For years, I resisted his presence.
I fought him and prevented his emergence.

I was my own fiercest enemy.

All that time, I heard his voice,
his requests, his longings, his desires, even his protests.
But more often than not, I either ignored him or stood firmly against him.
Though at times, he would win.
I could even say that most of the time, he did.

Without realizing it, it seems I ultimately followed his path and pursued his goals, with one difference: everything was heavily suppressed, weakened, and expressed cautiously.

Now, lately, I have been deliberately trying to face this different inner side of myself.
I am trying intensely to make peace with him, to offer friendship, to show goodwill, and to make clear that there is now space for his presence and expression.

In truth, I am begging forgiveness from him, pleading for his return.

Please come back, and this time take full control.
I surrender, entirely obedient.

I regret all the harshness and constant obstacles I created for you.
I never fully trusted you, yet you were always right, or at least there was more joy and satisfaction in your desires and ideas.
More valuable experiences.
More enjoyment of life’s path.

I am remorseful, and now, in whatever way I can, I am trying to create space and regain the trust of this inner self, hoping I may be forgiven, hoping he may finally emerge with clarity and force.

I hope he does.

Never before have I been this eager to know and witness this inner self, and never before have I felt this ashamed and regretful before myself.

A sincere request for forgiveness from oneself.

I wonder whether others have felt something similar.

The path to reaching this point has been long, winding, and fascinating.
Had I consciously documented it, I might now have a thick and detailed book ready for publication.

It began with conflict against society, against the compulsory conditions of life, geography, politics, history, and everything beyond my control.

Gradually, it narrowed toward smaller territories: the city, the home, the family, the circle of friends.

The search for the root of inner pain, the guilty party, the cause of dissatisfaction, seemed to move from vast external scales toward narrower and narrower spaces, until now it has landed fully and decisively upon the self.

My constant struggle with society, country, circumstance, my enduring anger, and my belief that all dissatisfaction came from the tyranny of geography, eventually led to discovering familial faults, methods of upbringing, traumas, and unconscious mistakes from my mother and father that left deep inner wounds.

And finally, perhaps over the last year, I reached this point and understood:

All of that may be true.
But whatever exists, in essence and in the end, rises from within me.

I was no longer at war with external forces.
I no longer held indescribable rage toward society or geography.
I no longer carried inner bitterness toward my mother and her mistakes in raising me.

There was no one left to blame.

Not because those things were meaningless or without effect, no.
But because understanding their influence allowed me to see the course of life more realistically, to accept those forces, and to make peace with them within myself.

I still protest all that was unjust and wrong that happened to me and to us.
But I no longer seek battle.

For about a year now, that war transformed into an exhausting battle between myself and myself.

A struggle I fell into unwillingly, and the more I tried to control or understand it, the more unsuccessful I became.

Every complaint, every objection, every small or large dissatisfaction, I redirected immediately back toward myself and assumed myself to be the cause.

It is still somewhat like that.

Yet somehow, I do not know how or why,
it seems this battle may be ending.

At least I hope it is.

A battle I knew from the first day could have no victor and no loser, because both sides were me.

And now I see that all the ideas, all the works and projects I unconsciously created or imagined, were attempts to uncover this inner conflict, ways of becoming aware of what was happening inside.

Now the dots have connected.
A clearer image has formed.

Now it seems I must try to observe the image, to know it.

I was my own fiercest enemy.

An enemy not yet brought to its knees, yet one that has only now realized that the one it has been fighting all these years… was itself.

A painful and dramatic moment.

An image of the self standing before the self, both stunned, wondering why they treated each other this way for so many years.
